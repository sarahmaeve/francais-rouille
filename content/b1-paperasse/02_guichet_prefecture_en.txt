At the Prefecture Counter

Characters:
- Ms. Dubreuil — a reception agent at the Loire-Atlantique prefecture
- Maeve Carrick — the American resident, there to renew her residence permit

Ms. Dubreuil: Good morning, madam, good morning. Do you have your appointment slip?

Maeve: Good morning. Yes, here it is, and I also printed the confirmation email.

Ms. Dubreuil: Perfect. Ms. Carrick, Maeve Carrick. Residence-permit renewal. All right. We'll go through your file together, in order, please. Valid passport, first.

Maeve: Here.

Ms. Dubreuil: Thank you. Previous residence permit?

Maeve: Yes, here you go.

Ms. Dubreuil: Very good. Proof of address, less than three months old, next.

Maeve: So, I brought my latest electricity bill.

Ms. Dubreuil: Let me see. Ah, I'm sorry, it's dated January eleventh. Today is April twenty-second. This bill is more than three months old. Unfortunately, it's not acceptable.

Maeve: Oh, I didn't know — I thought the last payment date counted. What can I bring instead?

Ms. Dubreuil: We need a recent proof — that is, less than three months old. That can be an electricity, gas, water or internet bill, or a rent receipt issued by your landlord.

Maeve: I have my tax notice, would that work?

Ms. Dubreuil: No, unfortunately. The tax notice is accepted as a supplementary document, but not as the main proof of address for the residence permit. I really need a current bill or a rent receipt.

Maeve: And my rental contract?

Ms. Dubreuil: The rental contract won't do either, because it was signed more than a year ago. What we'd need is either a recent attestation from your landlord, or a rent receipt dated from last month, or else a recent internet bill.

Maeve: All right. I think I can download my latest Free bill from my account area.

Ms. Dubreuil: Very good. If you can download it, print it, and come back, we can complete the file. The bill has to be in your name, with the exact address shown.

Maeve: OK. For the rest of the file, is everything fine?

Ms. Dubreuil: Let's see. Three regulation ID photos?

Maeve: Yes.

Ms. Dubreuil: Fine. They're compliant. Proof of income?

Maeve: Yes, my work contract, my last three payslips, and the France Travail certificate.

Ms. Dubreuil: Why France Travail — have you been unemployed?

Maeve: No, it's a non-registration certificate, which was listed as a required document.

Ms. Dubreuil: Ah, very good. That's correct. Perfect, everything is there on that side. So all I'm missing is the recent proof of address. Here's what I propose: I'll give you a receipt certifying the deposit of your file as of today. This receipt proves that your application is in progress, and it serves as a provisional residence authorisation. It's valid for six months.

Maeve: Oh, great, thanks. So I can continue working normally with this document?

Ms. Dubreuil: Yes, absolutely. You can work, travel in the Schengen area, and do everything you did before. However, it's essential that you complete your file quickly. I'll schedule a new appointment for you in a fortnight. Does May sixth, at ten a.m., work for you?

Maeve: Yes, perfectly.

Ms. Dubreuil: Very good, I'm booking it. You'll receive the confirmation by email later today. For this appointment, you only need to bring the missing proof of address. Everything else, I've already scanned.

Maeve: OK. And if I can't print the Free bill, is there another way?

Ms. Dubreuil: Yes. You can come with the bill on your phone, and we print it here, at the counter. It happens often. Or else, you can ask your landlord for a simple dated attestation of residence, on plain paper.

Maeve: Perfect. Thank you for your patience.

Ms. Dubreuil: You're welcome. Here — your receipt, your passport, your old permit. Your appointment slip for May sixth is in the email you'll receive.

Maeve: Thank you very much. Have a good day, madam.

Ms. Dubreuil: Good day to you. Next, please!
