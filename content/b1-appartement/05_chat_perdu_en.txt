The Lost Cat

Characters:
- Maeve — an American tenant who lives on the third floor
- Irene — Maeve's partner
- Hélène — a neighbor from the second floor, retired
- Karim — a neighbor from the ground floor, a student


Maeve : Hélène! Karim! Sorry to bother you, but have you seen a gray cat this morning? Ours has disappeared.

Hélène : A gray cat? What does it look like exactly?

Irene : He's dark gray with a small white spot on his chest. His name is Moustache. He got out this morning through the kitchen window and he hasn't come back.

Hélène : Oh, the poor thing! No, I haven't seen him today. But you know, the neighborhood cats often wander around on the rooftops. This is Croix-Rousse — there are passageways everywhere.

Karim : I saw a gray cat last night in the courtyard, around ten p.m. It was on the back wall. Maybe it's yours?

Maeve : Maybe! He loves climbing on walls. We searched the whole apartment, the stairwell, the basement, but nothing.

Karim : Have you looked in the traboules? There's one right next to the building. Cats love hiding in there.

Irene : The traboules — those are the covered passageways, right? We haven't thought of looking there yet.

Hélène : Yes, try the one that opens onto the montée de la Grande-Côte. Also, put his litter box on the balcony. Apparently the smell helps them find their way back.

Maeve : Good idea! We'll do that right away.

Karim : Otherwise, you can post on the neighborhood Facebook group. It's called "Habitants de la Croix-Rousse." People are super responsive on there. My neighbor found her cat that way last week.

Irene : Oh yeah, that's smart. Can you send us the link, Karim?

Karim : Yeah, I'll text it to you. Put a photo of Moustache with your number. It'll go fast.

Hélène : And I'll tell the concierge. She sees everything that goes on in the building. If your cat is somewhere in the common areas, she'll find him.

Maeve : Thank you both so much. We're really worried.

Hélène : Don't worry, sweetie. Cats always come back. My old cat used to disappear for three days and come back like nothing happened.

Karim : Yeah, don't stress too much. He's probably doing the rounds on the rooftops. He'll come back when he's hungry!

Irene : I hope you're right. Thanks for your help — it really means a lot.
