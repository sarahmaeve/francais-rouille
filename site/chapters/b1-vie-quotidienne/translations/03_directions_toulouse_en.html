Asking for Directions in Toulouse

B1 Dialogue — English Translation

Characters

  • Nadia — a student who has just arrived in Toulouse
  • Pierre — a local resident
Nadia: Excuse me, sir, I'm a bit lost. Do you know the Place du Capitole?
Pierre: Yes, of course, it's not very far from here. Are you on foot?
Nadia: Yes, I prefer to walk to discover the city. About how many minutes is it?
Pierre: On foot, it's about ten minutes. Continue straight ahead on this street until the end.
Nadia: Okay, straight ahead. And then?
Pierre: Then, you'll reach a large intersection. Turn left onto the rue d'Alsace-Lorraine.
Nadia: The rue d'Alsace-Lorraine — is it a big street?
Pierre: Yes, it's a well-known shopping street. There are a lot of shops — you can't miss it.
Nadia: Very good. And I continue on that street?
Pierre: Yes, you follow it for about five minutes. You'll pass a church on your right.
Nadia: And the Place du Capitole is right after that?
Pierre: Almost. After the church, take the second street on the right. You'll come out directly onto the square.
Nadia: Great, that sounds simple. Are there places to eat on the square?
Pierre: There are plenty of restaurants and cafés all around. But be careful — the ones on the square are a bit expensive. I'd recommend the little streets just behind it.
Nadia: Thank you so much for your advice! That's really kind.
Pierre: You're welcome, welcome to Toulouse! You'll see — it's a very pleasant city.
