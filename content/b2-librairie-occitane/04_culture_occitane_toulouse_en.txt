Occitan Culture in Toulouse

Characters:
- Irene — an American tourist visiting Toulouse
- Arnaud — the owner of the Librariá La Tuta d'Òc

Irene : Arnaud, is your bookstore the only place in Toulouse where you can discover Occitan culture?

Arnaud : Oh no, far from it! Occitan culture is present throughout the city, even if you don't always notice it. Have you seen the bilingual street signs?

Irene : Now that you mention it, yes! I'd noticed a second name under some street names, but I thought it was Spanish.

Arnaud : That's Occitan. The city hall installed those signs a few years ago. If you look closely, you'll also notice that the metro announces stations in both French and Occitan. It's subtle, but it's there.

Irene : And apart from the signs, are there other places to visit?

Arnaud : Of course. The Ostal d'Occitània is a cultural center dedicated to the Occitan language and culture. They organize language classes, concerts, exhibitions. It's in the city center, not far from here.

Irene : And music? You mentioned artists earlier.

Arnaud : Yes, Toulouse is a real hub for contemporary Occitan music. The Fabulous Trobadors, for example, blend Occitan with hip-hop and reggae. There are also more traditional groups who play Occitan folk music with ancient instruments.

Irene : That's surprising — hip-hop in Occitan!

Arnaud : That's exactly what keeps a language alive — when young people take ownership of it and make something modern out of it. We also have visual artists who work on Occitan identity. In fact, here in the bookstore, we regularly exhibit works by local artists.

Irene : I noticed the paintings when I came in, yes. They're beautiful.

Arnaud : Those are screen prints by Marta Gimenez, a Toulouse artist of Catalan origin. She works on the connections between Occitan and Catalan — the two languages are cousins, you know. Her works blend Occitan calligraphy with modern geometric patterns.

Irene : That's really beautiful. And are there festivals or events celebrating this culture?

Arnaud : Yes, the festival Occitània takes place every summer. For a week, there are concerts, theater in Occitan, lectures, traditional cooking workshops. It's the big annual gathering for lovers of the culture d'oc. If you come back in July, I highly recommend it.
