# The Fiber Optic Installation

**Characters:**
- **Irene** — Maeve's partner, a tenant on the third floor
- **Monsieur Reynaud** — the president of the building management association

---

**Irene:** Hello, Monsieur Reynaud. Sorry to bother you. Would you have a few minutes?

**Monsieur Reynaud:** Hello, madam. Yes, of course, go ahead.

**Irene:** Well, my partner and I would like to have fiber optic installed in our apartment. We contacted Orange, and a technician could come next week. However, they told us we need authorization from the building management to run the cable through the common areas.

**Monsieur Reynaud:** Yes, indeed, that's the procedure. The cable has to go through the building's riser, and any work in the common areas requires approval.

**Irene:** Would it be possible to get this authorization quickly? We both work from home, and our current ADSL connection is really inadequate.

**Monsieur Reynaud:** I completely understand. In principle, the law is on your side. The co-owners cannot oppose the installation of fiber for a tenant. It's the right to fiber — it's written in the law.

**Irene:** Ah, that's reassuring. So, what are the steps to follow?

**Monsieur Reynaud:** Your provider — Orange in your case — would need to send us an official letter describing the planned work. Then I pass it on to the building council, and normally we give approval within two weeks.

**Irene:** Two weeks? Is that the usual timeframe?

**Monsieur Reynaud:** Yes, that's the maximum. In practice, it's often faster. I can speed things up if the file is complete. I need the letter from Orange with the installation plan, and a copy of your lease.

**Irene:** Very well, I'll ask Orange to send the letter tomorrow. As for the lease, I can get you a copy this afternoon.

**Monsieur Reynaud:** Perfect. Bring it to my office — I'm here until six p.m. There's just one important point: the technician must be careful not to drill new holes in the load-bearing walls. He must use the existing cable runs.

**Irene:** I'll pass that instruction on to the technician. Is there anything else we need to know?

**Monsieur Reynaud:** No, that's all. Oh wait, one small thing: let your neighbors on the third floor know on the day of the installation, because the technician will need access to the landing and it might be a bit noisy. It's a matter of courtesy.

**Irene:** Of course, we'll let them know. Thank you very much, Monsieur Reynaud. I'll bring you the copy of the lease later today.

**Monsieur Reynaud:** You're welcome, madam. Have a good day.
