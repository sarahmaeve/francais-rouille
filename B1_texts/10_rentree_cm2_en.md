# Back to School in CM2 — The First Month

**Characters:**
- **Sandrine** — a parent waiting outside the school
- **Olivier** — a father picking up his daughter
- **Fatima** — a parent who lives in the neighborhood

---

**Sandrine:** Ah, hello Olivier! Hello Fatima! It's lovely to see you. So, how are things going for your children? The first month in CM2 is a big change, isn't it?

**Olivier:** Hello! Yes, my daughter Chloé was a bit stressed at the beginning. She was afraid of having a stricter teacher than last year.

**Fatima:** It's the same for my son Adam. He didn't know many students in his class this year, so he was worried.

**Sandrine:** Lucas too — he didn't want to go the first week. But now he loves his teacher. She organizes group projects, and he really enjoys that.

**Olivier:** Chloé is also starting to adjust. What surprised her is the amount of homework. In CM1, she had half an hour of work in the evening, but now it's nearly an hour.

**Fatima:** Oh yes, homework! Adam spends a lot of time on math. Fractions and geometry problems are new for him. He asks me for help every evening.

**Sandrine:** Lucas has the same problem with verb conjugation. The passé simple, the subjunctive... He finds it very difficult. I bought him a little exercise workbook to practice.

**Olivier:** That's a good idea. Chloé loves science. Her teacher does experiments in class, and last week they studied the solar system. She talks about it all the time at home.

**Fatima:** Adam likes science too! On the other hand, he struggles with dictation. Spelling is really his weak point. Does the teacher do dictations every week for you as well?

**Sandrine:** Yes, every Friday. Lucas got seven out of ten last week. He was proud of himself. But what worries me a little is next year. Middle school is a whole different world.

**Olivier:** I think about it too. Chloé is already asking me questions about middle school. She wants to know if she'll keep her friends, how it works with different teachers...

**Fatima:** I wonder if Adam will be ready. In CM2, they're the oldest in the school. In middle school, they go back to being the youngest. It's a huge change.

**Sandrine:** That's true. But there are still nine months to prepare them. And from what I can see, children adapt quickly. Lucas has already made two new friends this month.

**Olivier:** Chloé too. She signed up for the school drama workshop, and she loves it. It gives her confidence.

**Fatima:** That's great. Adam does the computer workshop. He's learning to program with Scratch. He's so happy that he wants to show me his projects every evening!

**Sandrine:** It's wonderful that there are so many activities. Well, here they come! Shall we meet up again next week?

**Olivier:** Gladly! Have a good evening, both of you.

**Fatima:** Good evening! And good luck with the homework tonight!
