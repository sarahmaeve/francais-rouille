Phone Call — Inquiring About the Apartment

B1 — English Translation

Characters

  • Camille — a young professional looking for an apartment
  • Thomas — a real estate agent at Belleville Real Estate Agency
Thomas: Belleville Real Estate Agency, hello! Thomas speaking — how can I help you?
Camille: Hello, I'm calling about the listing for the one-bedroom on rue de la Roquette. Is the apartment still available?
Thomas: Yes, absolutely, it's still available. Do you have any questions?
Camille: Yes, several. First, is the apartment furnished or unfurnished?
Thomas: It's rented unfurnished. However, the kitchen is fully fitted, as stated in the listing.
Camille: Okay. And what's the neighborhood like? I don't know the 11th very well.
Thomas: It's an excellent neighborhood. Rue de la Roquette is very well located, between Bastille and Voltaire. There's everything you need nearby: supermarkets, pharmacies, bakeries.
Camille: And what about the noise? I read that rue de la Roquette is quite lively in the evenings.
Thomas: It's true that the lower part of the street is lively with bars and restaurants. But the apartment is higher up on the street — it's much quieter. And the bedroom faces the courtyard, so you hear almost nothing.
Camille: Ah, that's reassuring. Are there any green spaces nearby?
Thomas: Yes, the Square Maurice-Gardette is just a few minutes away. And if you like to go for walks, Père-Lachaise cemetery is very pleasant — many Parisians go there to walk in peace and quiet.
Camille: Very good. Is it convenient for transportation?
Thomas: Very convenient. Voltaire is five minutes on foot, and Bastille is ten minutes. With Bastille, you have three metro lines, so you can get anywhere quickly.
Camille: Perfect. And for the application, what do I need to provide?
Thomas: You'll need your last three pay slips, your employment contract, an ID document, and your most recent tax notice. Your income must be at least three times the rent amount.
Camille: Okay, I can prepare all of that. Could I visit the apartment this week?
Thomas: Of course. I can offer you Thursday at 2 PM or Friday at 10 AM. What works for you?
Camille: Friday at 10 AM would be perfect for me.
Thomas: Noted. We'll meet in front of the building, at 87 rue de la Roquette. You'll see the keypad — I'll open the door for you.
Camille: Thank you very much, Thomas. See you Friday then!
Thomas: See you Friday, Camille. Have a good day!
