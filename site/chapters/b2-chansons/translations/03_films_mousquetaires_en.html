The Three Musketeers at the Movies

B2 — English Translation

Characters

  • Maeve — an American student living in Lyon, an enthusiastic film lover
  • Irene — an American student living in Lyon, more critical of French cinema
Irene: Did you end up watching "Milady"? Because I saw it last week and I have things to say.
Maeve: Yes! I watched it Friday night. So, what did you think?
Irene: Honestly? I found it just as dark as the first one. "D'Artagnan" had already disappointed me a bit with its tone — it's supposed to be a story of adventure, of panache, and in the end you get something super serious, almost depressing at times.
Maeve: That's funny, because the dark side didn't bother me. I thought it gave the story weight. It's not a Marvel movie where everything is light and consequence-free.
Irene: Sure, but there's a middle ground, you know. At its core, Dumas is serialized fiction, it's entertainment. The characters are supposed to be flamboyant, funny, a bit boastful. In the films, it's like they carry the weight of the world on their shoulders.
Maeve: I'll give you that for D'Artagnan. François Civil is great, but his character lacks lightness. On the other hand, the action scenes are really well done. The duel in the church — remember that? It was spectacular.
Irene: Oh yes, the fight choreography is impressive. That's what kept me from tuning out completely. But between the action scenes, there are slow stretches. The political intrigues are poorly explained — if you haven't read the novel, you're lost.
Maeve: That's true, it's dense. But I'd read a summary beforehand, so I could follow along. And we have to talk about Eva Green. She's incredible as Milady. She's the best thing in both films.
Irene: We completely agree on that. Eva Green is perfect. Every scene she appears in, you can't look away. She has a screen presence that's just… mesmerizing. Even when the script is shaky, she holds you.
Maeve: And Milady is such a complex character. Villainous, yes, but with motivations you can understand. Eva Green plays all those nuances. She's a world-class actress.
Irene: Absolutely. Actually, I think the second film is a bit better than the first, precisely because there's more Milady. Her scenes with D'Artagnan have real tension.
Maeve: Yes, exactly. And the ending leaves the door open for a third film. Do you think they'll make it?
Irene: No idea. The reviews were decent but not glowing, and at the box office, it wasn't the triumph they'd hoped for. It did well in France, but internationally, it went somewhat unnoticed.
Maeve: It'd be a shame not to finish the trilogy. The story isn't over, and the costumes, the sets — it's still beautiful French cinema. We need more adventure films like this in France.
Irene: Maybe. But if it's going to be the same thing only even darker, I'm not sure I'll show up. Unless Eva Green is in it, obviously.
Maeve: Obviously. Without Eva Green, there's no point.
