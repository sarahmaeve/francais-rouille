HR Welcome — First Day in Nantes

Characters:
- Maeve Carrick — a young American designer, newly joining Atelier Clairac
- Anaïs Belmont — the HR manager, showing her around
- Ludo Pasquier — the CEO, whom they bump into in a corridor

Anaïs: Good morning, Maeve, welcome! You're right on time, that's perfect. I'm Anaïs, HR. We met on a video call, but it's nice to meet you in person.

Maeve: Good morning, Anaïs! Yes, lovely to meet you too. I was a bit worried about being late, with the tram.

Anaïs: Don't worry, everyone arrives between nine and half past, it's very flexible. So — we'll do a quick tour, I'll give you your badge and your laptop, and we'll go through a couple of practical things. Shall we?

Maeve: Yes, of course.

Anaïs: So here, right by the entrance, is reception. You see — you tap your badge on the reader to open the door. For now, I'll open with mine, and we'll pick yours up in my office in a moment.

Maeve: Got it. And what else is the badge for?

Anaïs: It's for the doors, and also for the coffee machine, and the canteen. It's our Swiss Army knife, this badge.

Maeve: Oh, that's practical!

Anaïs: Here's the open space. We're about forty people in total, but not everyone is in this morning — some are working from home on Mondays. Your desk is there, by the window. Raphaël, whom you're about to meet, is right opposite.

Maeve: Oh, it's bright, it's lovely.

Anaïs: Yes, we're lucky. Over there at the back, that's the main meeting room, we call it "Loire". The smaller room over there is "Erdre". All our rooms are named after rivers, it's a little tradition of the house.

Maeve: That's nice. And where's the kitchen?

Anaïs: Right behind you. Come on, let's go. So — here's the kitchen-lounge. There's a fridge — everyone stores their things, and we clean it on Friday evenings, in turn. And there, the coffee machine. Watch out, it's a bean-to-cup machine, it's lethal, people drink far too much.

Maeve: Haha, I'll be careful.

Anaïs: How do you take your coffee?

Maeve: A long one, no sugar.

Anaïs: Then you press here, "Americano". There, easy. The terrace is out through the glass door. People have lunch out there as soon as it's sunny.

Maeve: Great. And how does lunch break work?

Anaïs: We have two options. Either you go to the collective canteen, which is a hundred metres down the street — there's an internal app to order in the morning, it's handy. Or you go to the restaurant on the corner, or you grab something quick here. You can also bring your own lunchbox.

Maeve: OK, and are lunch hours fixed?

Anaïs: No, it's flexible, generally between noon and two. The team often eats together around one, but nothing is required.

Anaïs: Right, let's continue. The toilets are at the end of the corridor on the right. Here's the break room, with a little sofa, that's where we go when we need a breather. And over there is my office. Let's head there for your equipment.

Anaïs: There we are. So, I'm giving you your access badge, your canteen card, and your laptop. The laptop is already set up. Your login for the work mailbox is maeve.carrick, and the temporary password is on the sheet. You'll change it in a bit.

Maeve: Great, thanks.

Anaïs: For the internal Wi-Fi, it's "AtelierClairac", password also on the sheet. The guest Wi-Fi you won't use, that's for visitors.

Ludo: Ah, Maeve! Welcome aboard!

Anaïs: Ludo, let me introduce you to Maeve, officially.

Ludo: Pleased to meet you, Maeve. I'm Ludo, the director. Use "tu" with me, we're pretty laid-back here. Did you have a good trip down from Lyon?

Maeve: Hello Ludo. Yes, very good, thanks. The TGV is fantastic.

Ludo: Perfect. Right, Anaïs will walk you through everything. Shall we catch up this afternoon? I suggest we grab a coffee at three, get to know each other a bit more quietly.

Maeve: With pleasure, see you later.

Ludo: See you later. Anaïs, you'll show her the bean-to-cup machine, yes? That's critical.

Anaïs: Already done, Ludo. Right, let's keep going. For timesheets, we don't have anything rigid, but you'll see — there's a little tool called Clockify on your laptop that we fill in rather at the end of the week, for project tracking.

Maeve: OK.

Anaïs: Raphaël will come and fetch you in ten minutes to bring you into his team. He'll take you through what they're working on. And at lunchtime, we've arranged for you to eat with some of the team at the canteen. You're expected, basically.

Maeve: Thank you, this is all very well organised.

Anaïs: We try! I'll leave you to settle in. If you have any questions at all, come find me — don't hesitate.

Maeve: Understood, thanks so much Anaïs.

Anaïs: See you later!
