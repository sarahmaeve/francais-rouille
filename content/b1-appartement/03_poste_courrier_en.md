# The Lost Registered Letter

**Characters:**
- **Maeve** — an Irish tenant who lives in the Croix-Rousse neighborhood
- **The clerk** — a postal worker handling several customers at once

---

**Maeve:** Hello, sir. I'm here about a registered letter I've been waiting for for three weeks. It's coming from the United States and it still hasn't arrived.

**The clerk:** Hello. Do you have the tracking number?

**Maeve:** Yes, here it is. It's a registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt.

**The clerk:** All right. Hold on, let me check... So, the parcel left the United States on February 18th. It went through customs at Roissy on the 25th. After that, I have nothing more in the system.

**Maeve:** Nothing more? But what does that mean? Is it lost?

**The clerk:** Not necessarily. It could be held up at customs, or waiting to be sorted at a distribution center. It happens with international shipments, especially right now — we're running behind.

**Maeve:** But three weeks is still a very long time, isn't it? These are important documents. I need them for an administrative procedure.

**The clerk:** I understand, ma'am. But there's not much I can do from my window. You would need to file a complaint online on the La Poste website. Do you have an account?

**Maeve:** No, not yet. How do I do that?

**The clerk:** You go to laposte.fr, you create an account, and then you go to "Help and Complaints." You enter the tracking number and describe the problem.

**Maeve:** Okay. And then what happens?

**The clerk:** They open an investigation. Generally, they respond within ten business days. If the letter is truly lost, you can request compensation, but the sender also has to file a claim on their end.

**Maeve:** Ten days? That's a long time. Isn't there a way to speed things up?

**The clerk:** Unfortunately not — that's the procedure. You can also call 3631, that's customer service. But the timeframes are the same. Excuse me, there are people waiting behind you. Anything else?

**Maeve:** No, that's all. Thanks anyway.

**The clerk:** You're welcome. Have a good day, ma'am. Next, please!
