The Fiber Optic Installation

B1 Dialogue — English Translation

Characters

  • Irene — Maeve's partner, tenant on the third floor
  • Monsieur Reynaud — president of the building management
Irene: Hello M. Reynaud. Sorry to bother you. Would you have a few minutes?
M. Reynaud: Hello madam. Yes of course, go ahead.
Irene: My partner and I would like fiber optic installed. We contacted Orange, and a technician could come next week. But they said we need authorization from the building management for the cable through the common areas.
M. Reynaud: Yes indeed, that's the procedure. The cable goes through the building riser, and any work in the common areas requires approval.
Irene: Would it be possible to get authorization quickly? We both work from home, and our ADSL connection is really inadequate.
M. Reynaud: I completely understand. The law is on your side. Co-owners cannot oppose fiber installation for a tenant. It's the right to fiber — it's in the law.
Irene: That's reassuring. What are the steps?
M. Reynaud: Your provider needs to send us an official letter describing the planned work. I pass it to the building council, and normally approval comes within two weeks.
Irene: Two weeks? Is that usual?
M. Reynaud: Maximum. In practice, often faster. I can speed things up if the file is complete. I need the letter from Orange with the installation plan and a copy of your lease.
Irene: I'll ask Orange to send the letter tomorrow. For the lease, I can get you a copy this afternoon.
M. Reynaud: Perfect. Bring it to my office, I'm here until 6pm. One important point: the technician must not drill new holes in the load-bearing walls. He must use the existing cable runs.
Irene: I'll pass that on. Anything else we should know?
M. Reynaud: No, that's all. Oh wait — let your 3rd floor neighbors know on installation day, the technician needs landing access and it might be noisy. Matter of courtesy.
Irene: Of course, we'll let them know. Thank you very much M. Reynaud. I'll bring the lease copy later today.
M. Reynaud: You're welcome madam. Have a good day.
