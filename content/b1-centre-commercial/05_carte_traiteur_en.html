Traiteur Menu — Ruellan

A guide to understanding a French traiteur menu

A traiteur is a French prepared-food shop — somewhere between a deli and a caterer. Unlike a restaurant, you typically order at the counter and take your food away, though some traiteurs have a small seating area. The dishes are freshly made by professional cooks and are generally higher quality than supermarket ready meals.

Menu Categories Explained

Pièces apéritives — Small bite-sized appetizers, often ordered in quantity for parties or gatherings. Tartines are open-faced toasts; verrines are layered appetizers served in small glasses; bun's are small filled buns.

Entrées froides / chaudes — Starters, either cold or hot. An ardoise de charcuterie is a slate board of cured meats. A bouchée à la Reine is a puff pastry shell filled with a creamy sauce of chicken, mushrooms, and sometimes sweetbreads — a classic French dish.

Poissons cuisinés — Cooked fish dishes. Cabillaud is cod, lieu jaune is pollack, bar is sea bass, and turbot is turbot. Saint-Jacques are scallops — a prized specialty of Brittany. Note the minimum of 4 portions — traiteur dishes are often prepared in batches.

Viandes cuisinées — Cooked meat dishes. A filet de bœuf Rossini is a fillet topped with foie gras and truffle — named after the composer, who was a famous gourmand. Pluma ibérique is a tender cut from Iberian pork. Suprême de volaille is a skin-on chicken breast.

Autour d'un plat — Literally "around a dish," these are hearty, often internationally influenced one-pot meals. Colombo is a Caribbean-style curry popular in the French Antilles. Parmentier refers to any dish topped with mashed potato (like shepherd's pie) — named after Antoine Parmentier, who popularized potatoes in France.

Accompagnements — Side dishes, priced per kilogram. Gratin dauphinois is the classic potato gratin from the Dauphiné region — sliced potatoes baked in cream. Girolles are chanterelle mushrooms.

Desserts — Île flottante (floating island) is meringue floating on custard cream. Flan parisien is a firm egg custard tart, a Parisian staple. Entremets are layered mousse cakes that change with the season.
