The Lost Registered Letter

B1 Dialogue — English Translation

Characters

  • Maeve — an Irish tenant living in the Croix-Rousse neighborhood
  • The clerk — a postal worker handling several customers
Maeve: Hello sir. I'm here about a registered letter I've been waiting for for three weeks. From the US, still hasn't arrived.
The clerk: Hello. Do you have the tracking number?
Maeve: Yes, here it is. Registered letter with acknowledgment of receipt.
The clerk: All right. Hold on... The parcel left the US Feb 18. Went through customs at Roissy on the 25th. After that, nothing in the system.
Maeve: Nothing? What does that mean? Is it lost?
The clerk: Not necessarily. Could be held at customs, or waiting at a sorting center. Happens with international mail, especially now — we're running behind.
Maeve: But three weeks is very long. These are important documents for an administrative procedure.
The clerk: I understand, but there's not much I can do from my window. You'd need to file a complaint online on the La Poste website. Do you have an account?
Maeve: No, not yet. How do I do that?
The clerk: Go to laposte.fr, create an account, go to "Help and Complaints." Enter tracking number and describe the problem.
Maeve: Okay. And then what happens?
The clerk: They open an investigation. Usually respond within ten business days. If truly lost, you can request compensation, but the sender must also file on their end.
Maeve: Ten days? That's long. Isn't there a way to speed things up?
The clerk: Unfortunately not — that's the procedure. You can also call 3631, customer service. But timeframes are the same. Excuse me, people are waiting. Anything else?
Maeve: No, that's all. Thanks anyway.
The clerk: You're welcome. Have a good day. Next please!
