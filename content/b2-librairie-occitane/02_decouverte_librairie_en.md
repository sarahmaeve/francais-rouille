# Discovering the Bookstore

**Characters:**
- **Irene** — an American tourist visiting Toulouse
- **Arnaud** — the owner of the Librariá La Tuta d'Òc

**Irene:** Hello! I saw your storefront while walking down the street. It's a bookstore in… that's not French, is it?

**Arnaud:** Hello! No, it's not French. It's Occitan, the historic language of this region. Welcome to La Tuta d'Òc — it means "everything in the language of oc."

**Irene:** Occitan? I didn't even know this language existed. I thought everyone in France spoke French.

**Arnaud:** That's what people often think abroad. But before French became the official language, people here spoke Occitan. It was the everyday language — at the market, at church, in families. And it was also a great literary language.

**Irene:** Really? How long has it been around?

**Arnaud:** Since the Middle Ages at least. The troubadours — have you heard of them? Those poets of the 12th and 13th centuries who invented courtly love poetry — they wrote in Occitan, not French.

**Irene:** The troubadours! Yes, I've heard of them, but I thought they wrote in Old French.

**Arnaud:** No, that's a very common mix-up. Their language was the langue d'oc. Northern French was called the langue d'oïl. They were two different languages, as different as Spanish and Portuguese.

**Irene:** And today, are there still people who speak Occitan?

**Arnaud:** Yes, but far fewer than before. The language was gradually replaced by French, especially after the Revolution. But there's a revival movement — bilingual schools, cultural associations, artists. That's why this bookstore exists.

**Irene:** That's fascinating. I had no idea about any of this. And you — do you speak Occitan?

**Arnaud:** Of course! My grandmother only spoke Occitan. I grew up with both languages. For me, keeping this bookstore open is a way of keeping my ancestors' culture alive.

**Irene:** That's admirable. Do you have any books in French about this history? I'd love to learn more.

**Arnaud:** Absolutely. Come, I'll show you our section on the history of the langue d'oc. We also have bilingual Occitan-French books that are perfect for discovering the language.
