Discussing the Neighborhood

B1 Dialogue — English Translation (Colloquial Parisian French)

Characters

  • Camille — the prospective renter, visiting the neighborhood
  • Yasmine — a resident of the neighborhood for five years, met at a café
Camille: Excuse me, can I ask you a question? I'm thinking about moving to the area and I wanted to know what it's like to live here.
Yasmine: Oh yeah, you're looking in the neighborhood? I've been living here for five years — honestly, it's great.
Camille: Really? What do you like the most?
Yasmine: Well, first of all there's an amazing vibe. It's lively — there's always something going on. And people are friendly — it's not like some neighborhoods where nobody talks to each other.
Camille: And for grocery shopping, is it good?
Yasmine: Oh, totally! There's the Bastille market on Thursdays and Sundays — it's awesome. The fruits and vegetables are super fresh, and there's a cheese seller there who's incredible. Otherwise there's a Franprix on the corner that stays open late — that's handy.
Camille: And what about restaurants? I saw there are quite a few in the area.
Yasmine: Yeah, there are tons. If you like to eat well without breaking the bank, there are some little spots on rue Keller and rue de Charonne that are really nice. Avoid the tourist traps around Bastille though — the food's not great and it's expensive.
Camille: Good to know. And in the evening, is it noisy? The agent told me it's quiet at the top of la Roquette, but still...
Yasmine: No, but he's right, you know. At the bottom it's party central on weekends, for sure. But above rue de Charonne, it's peaceful. I sleep with the windows open in summer — zero problems.
Camille: That's reassuring. And do you find it safe in the evening?
Yasmine: Yeah, honestly, I've never had a problem. There are people in the streets until pretty late, so you don't feel alone. It's a neighborhood that's alive, you know?
Camille: Are there nice places to go for a walk on weekends?
Yasmine: Totally! Père-Lachaise is right next door — it's gorgeous and super quiet. And the Canal Saint-Martin isn't far either — it's perfect for a chill Sunday. People have picnics by the water — it's relaxing.
Camille: That sounds wonderful! I think I'm going to like it here.
Yasmine: Oh, for sure. Once you're in the 11th, you never want to leave. It's kind of like a village inside Paris — you'll see.
Camille: Thank you so much, it's really nice of you to take the time to chat with me!
Yasmine: Don't mention it! And if you move in, come grab a coffee here — it's my hangout. Welcome to the neighborhood!
