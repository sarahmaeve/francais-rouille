Lunch at the Canteen

B1 — English Translation

Characters

  • Maeve Carrick — the new American designer
  • Raphaël Noguès — a colleague designer, slightly shy, twenty-nine
  • Camille Perret — a senior designer, funny and direct, thirty-five
  • Sébastien Roy — a laid-back developer, thirty-eight
Raphaël: So, Maeve, you survived Anaïs's guided tour?
Maeve: I survived, yes! It's a lot of information in one morning, but everything is clearly explained. The badge, the rivers, the bean-to-cup machine...
Camille: The bean-to-cup is the onboarding trial. If you haven't burnt your fingers on it at least once during your first week, you're not really one of us.
Maeve: Haha, I've been warned!
Sébastien: Right, what are we having? Plat du jour is coley in beurre blanc. I'll go for that.
Raphaël: I'm going to the salad bar, I need something green today.
Camille: It's Wednesday, there's the Italian buffet. I'll have pasta with tomato, that'll do me. You, Maeve?
Maeve: I'm looking. What exactly is coley?
Sébastien: It's a fish, similar to cod but with a slightly stronger taste. It's pretty good, actually.
Maeve: OK, I'll have the fish too. Beurre blanc is very Nantes, isn't it?
Camille: Completely. It's the signature sauce here. If you've never tasted it, now's the time.
Maeve: Perfect. Let's go.
Camille: So, Maeve, important first question. Where are you living in Nantes?
Maeve: For now, I'm in a short-term furnished rental in the centre, around Graslin. But I'll be looking for something more stable from next month.
Sébastien: Graslin is nice but expensive. What kind of neighbourhood are you aiming for in your real search?
Maeve: I'm not sure yet. I like being close to the tram, to get to work without a car. And I want somewhere lively, but not crazy — I like sleeping at night.
Raphaël: Les Hauts-Pavés is a good compromise. It's a bit further north, it's lively, there are shops, there are quiet bars, and the tram is right there.
Camille: Yeah, Hauts-Pavés, or even the Île de Nantes. The island is newer, more modern. The rents are a bit higher, but you have the Loire right there, and the Machines de l'île, and Hangar à Bananes at the weekend.
Maeve: Hangar à Bananes?
Sébastien: It's a former dock — they turned it into a zone of bars and restaurants by the water. In summer, it's busy, it's nice.
Camille: Avoid Malakoff, though. Or rather — don't avoid it, it's still Nantes — but for a first flat, I'd recommend against it, it's a slightly less pleasant area.
Maeve: Noted, thanks. And where do all of you live?
Raphaël: Me? Still with my parents. Shame on me. I've been looking for six months, but honestly, the rents...
Camille: I'm in Bouffay, the historic centre. It's super handy for going out. On the downside, it's noisy on Friday nights, I sleep with earplugs.
Sébastien: I live in Rezé, just across the Loire. It's basically the suburbs of Nantes, but it's nice — I have a little garden, and it's five tram stops.
Camille: Another really important question: Maeve, do you do any sport?
Maeve: Yes, I like running, and I do yoga once a week when I remember.
Raphaël: Oh, perfect! We have a friendly football match between colleagues every Thursday evening, on Île de Nantes, on the pitch next to the warehouse. It's mixed, very relaxed, not competitive at all — it's mostly for fun.
Camille: Mostly for the post-game beer, you mean.
Raphaël: That's part of the activity, yes.
Maeve: I'd love to try. I'm not a great footballer, but I can run.
Sébastien: Honestly, you're already better equipped than I was on my first match.
Camille: By the way, did you see the Garonne Énergie brief yesterday? The client wants to redo everything, again.
Raphaël: Oh no, seriously?
Camille: Yes, he sent a mail at ten p.m. last night. A classic. Basically, he wants "more impact", "more modern", but he can't tell us what that means.
Sébastien: Ah, we're in vague-feedback season.
Maeve: So what do you do in that case?
Camille: We propose three different visual directions, and let him choose. That way, he owns the decision, and we take back control. It's a classic move in this business.
Raphaël: It's a decent trick. Works eight times out of ten.
Sébastien: And the other two, he calls you back on a Friday at five p.m. with a new idea.
Everyone: Ha ha ha.
Camille: Right, shall we head back? I've got a call at two.
Raphaël: Catch up with you — I'm just popping to the loo.
Sébastien: Have a good afternoon, Maeve. Welcome, genuinely.
Maeve: Thanks! See you later.
