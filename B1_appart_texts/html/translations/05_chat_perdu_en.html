The Lost Cat

B1 Dialogue — English Translation

Characters

  • Maeve — an Irish tenant who lives on the third floor
  • Irene — Maeve's partner
  • Hélène — a neighbor from the second floor, retired
  • Karim — a neighbor from the ground floor, student
Maeve: Hélène! Karim! Sorry to bother you, but have you seen a gray cat this morning? Ours has disappeared.
Hélène: A gray cat? What does it look like exactly?
Irene: Dark gray with a small white spot on the chest. Named Moustache. He got out through the kitchen window this morning, hasn't come back.
Hélène: Oh poor thing! No, I haven't seen him. But neighborhood cats often wander on the rooftops. This is Croix-Rousse — passageways everywhere.
Karim: I saw a gray cat last night in the courtyard, around 10pm. On the back wall. Maybe yours?
Maeve: Maybe! He loves climbing walls. We searched the apartment, the stairwell, the basement — nothing.
Karim: Looked in the traboules? There's one right next to the building. Cats love hiding there.
Irene: Traboules — the covered passageways, right? Haven't thought of looking there.
Hélène: Yes, try the one onto montée de la Grande-Côte. Also put his litter box on the balcony — the smell helps them find their way back.
Maeve: Good idea! We'll do that right away.
Karim: You can post on the neighborhood Facebook group "Habitants de la Croix-Rousse." People are super responsive. My neighbor found her cat that way last week.
Irene: Smart. Can you send us the link, Karim?
Karim: Yeah, I'll text it. Put a photo of Moustache with your number. It'll go fast.
Hélène: I'll tell the concierge. She sees everything. If your cat's in the common areas, she'll find him.
Maeve: Thank you both so much. We're really worried.
Hélène: Don't worry sweetie. Cats always come back. My old cat used to disappear for three days and come back like nothing happened.
Karim: Yeah, don't stress too much. Probably doing the rounds on the rooftops. He'll come back when he's hungry!
Irene: I hope you're right. Thanks for your help — it really means a lot.
