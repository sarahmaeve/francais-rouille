The Annual Performance Review

Characters:
- Clément Rivière — Pixelune's CEO, leading the annual review
- Nadia Lefort — now Senior Data-Viz Engineer at Pixelune for one year

Clément: Hi Nadia, come in, have a seat. Coffee, tea, anything?

Nadia: A glass of water would be lovely, thanks.

Clément: There you go. So — your annual review. A year goes fast, doesn't it? I re-read your objectives from last year last night, and everything you've delivered. How about we start with my feedback, then give you time to share yours, and then talk about what's next. Does that work?

Nadia: Perfect, yes.

Clément: Good. First thing: I want to tell you that your onboarding has genuinely been remarkable. You set, with Hélène and me, three big objectives for your first year — the overhaul of the time-series graph module, the technical mentoring of the new hire, and contributing to the product roadmap. On all three, we've more than hit the mark.

Nadia: Thank you, that's good to hear.

Clément: On the overhaul, what struck me was the rigour of your approach. You proposed the project back in January, and we shipped it by end of June, on time and with code quality that genuinely raised the bar for the team. Client feedback is excellent — we measured a twenty percent drop in rendering time on large datasets, which is real.

Nadia: Yes, on that project, what made the difference was that we took the time to run workshops with users up front. I'd learned a lot from my previous role, where I'd skipped exactly that step.

Clément: Exactly. On mentoring Julien, again, I think you've done very sensitive work. He told me last week that he'd made huge progress, and above all that he felt confident taking initiative. It's not easy to teach without smothering.

Nadia: Julien is solid — he's motivated, he asks the right questions. I tried to give him room while staying available when he needed it.

Clément: That said, I'd like us to talk also about what can grow. Nothing dramatic, we agree. Two main things. First, cross-functional communication. You're very rooted in engineering, but we sometimes notice — and it comes back to me — that your exchanges with product and sales could be smoother. You have a tendency to stay in your lane.

Nadia: Fair, I take that feedback. Concretely, what do you have in mind?

Clément: For instance, when there's a priority shift on the product side, we'd benefit from you joining those conversations earlier, to challenge or reshape the decision, rather than discovering it in sprint planning. I think there's room to grow in your presence in those moments.

Nadia: I see. It's true I tend to let Jean handle that, and focus on the technical piece. I'll work on it.

Clément: Second point: the lead posture. You mentor Julien very well, as I said. But the question that's going to come up over the next six months is how you position yourself in relation to a broader team. Your scope will probably expand.

Nadia: Actually, that's a point I wanted to raise. I wanted to talk to you about my progression, and the fact that I feel ready now to take on the role of lead engineer for the whole data-viz side. With, of course, a formal salary adjustment and an officially widened scope.

Clément: I was expecting you to ask, and I fully understand the request. I'll be frank: in principle, I'm in favour. On timing, we need to talk this through seriously. Marc is retiring in six months, and his succession is a real issue. So what I propose is that we build a clear plan for the next six months.

Nadia: Go on.

Clément: I propose we set three milestones. Within three months, you'll have taken over the data-viz team rituals, you'll be more strongly positioned in product conversations — we already discussed this — and you'll have completed a technical-leadership training, which I'll fund from the extended training budget. Within six months, you'll have run your first annual review for Julien, and you'll have presented your vision for the team at an internal seminar. At the end of those six months, if things go as I expect, we officially move you to the lead role, with a salary raise I'd put around ten percent of your base.

Nadia: That's ambitious but fair. I'll take it. One question, on the seminar — is it a set format, or can I propose something more original?

Clément: Carte blanche. Surprise us.

Nadia: Good. And on the training, do you have a recommendation or should I suggest something?

Clément: Suggest, and we'll discuss. There's a programme at Mines Paris that has a good reputation, I heard about it recently — but see what you find.

Nadia: All right. Thank you for framing this — it's very clear. I leave with visibility.

Clément: That's the idea. I suggest we do an informal check-in in three months, to measure progress. We'll of course write all this down formally.

Nadia: Perfect. Thanks Clément.

Clément: Thank you, Nadia. You're doing great work.
